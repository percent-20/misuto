The Solution

What if login security didn't require the user to do anything?

Misuto checks whether an employee is in a trusted physical environment. The right office, the right WiFi, the right time of day. That becomes the proof of identity. No codes. No prompts. The employee just logs in normally.

1

The employee's phone reads their surroundings

Which WiFi networks are nearby? What's the GPS location? What time is it? Are familiar Bluetooth devices in range? The phone collects this silently, in the background, with no user interaction.

2

That context becomes an encrypted, one-time environmental signature

The phone encrypts those signals into a short-lived environmental signature that proves "I'm in a trusted place right now." The raw data (actual WiFi names, exact GPS) never leaves the device.

3

Misuto checks it against what it expects

Does this environmental signature match the employee's known work environment? If yes, the login proceeds instantly. Wrong location, unusual time, or unfamiliar network? Access is blocked.

4

All of this happens before your identity provider is involved

Misuto sits in front of Okta, Entra, or Auth0. An attacker with a stolen password is stopped before the login page even loads. Your employees never notice it's there.

FAQ

Common questions

What exactly does Misuto check on the employee's phone?

The nearby WiFi networks (not their content, just their names), the phone's GPS location, the current time, and any Bluetooth devices in range. The combination of these signals creates a unique environmental signature that's extremely hard to fake from another location.

Does the employee need to install an app or do anything?

Employees install a lightweight companion app once during onboarding. After that, the app runs silently in the background. There are no daily interactions. No codes to type, no notifications to approve. Most employees forget it's there.

What happens if an employee works from home, a café, or while traveling?

Employees can register multiple trusted environments: home office, headquarters, co-working spaces, even a regular café. Each can have its own schedule (e.g., home office allowed 8am–6pm). When an employee moves to a new regular location, adding it takes seconds.

What happens if an employee loses their phone?

IT revokes the phone's access immediately from the admin console. When the employee gets a new phone, they install the app and re-enroll. Their trusted environments are restored automatically. No hardware to ship, no tokens to replace.

Does Misuto replace our existing MFA (Okta Verify, Microsoft Authenticator, etc.)?

It can, but it doesn't have to. Most enterprises start by running Misuto alongside their existing MFA in observation mode. Once confident, they gradually phase out push notifications and OTP codes. Misuto provides stronger verification with less user friction.

How long does deployment take?

Typically 2–4 weeks. The first phase runs Misuto in shadow mode alongside your existing login flow, verifying environments without blocking anyone, so you can validate accuracy. The second phase enables enforcement, starting with a pilot group and expanding from there.

What about compliance (NIST, SOC 2, GDPR)?

Misuto never stores raw location data or WiFi names on its servers. Only encrypted, irreversible proofs. This aligns with GDPR data minimization requirements and SOC 2 data handling controls. We provide detailed compliance documentation during the evaluation process.

What happens if Misuto's servers go down?

By default, Misuto fails closed. If it can't verify the environment, access is denied until service is restored. For business-critical apps, you can configure a fallback to your existing MFA so employees aren't locked out during an outage.
